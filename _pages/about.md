## Hey, I'm Taulant

I'm a Ph.D. candidate at the [Robust Data Science Group](https://www.etit.tu-darmstadt.de/rds/index.en.jsp) at TU Darmstadt. I spend most of my time thinking about how to make machine learning methods work on large, messy, high-dimensional data — and how to do so with some statistical rigor.

My research sits somewhere between machine learning and signal processing. I like problems where you need to figure out something clever algorithmically, not just throw more compute at it.

### What I Work On

- **High-dimensional variable selection** — figuring out which features actually matter when you have millions of them, with guarantees that you're not fooling yourself
- **Sparse signal recovery** — reconstructing signals when the measurements are shuffled or corrupted
- **Biomedical signals** — I've worked on ECG analysis using graph-based methods, and that work ended up in an open-source library ([neurokit2](https://github.com/neuropsychology/NeuroKit))

### How I Got Here

I did my B.Sc. and M.Sc. at TU Darmstadt in Electrical Engineering. Along the way I spent time at Aalto University in Finland and at EPFL in Switzerland, where I wrote my Master's thesis at the [LCAV lab](https://www.epfl.ch/labs/lcav/) with the [Swiss Data Science Center](https://datascience.ch).

After that I did a short stint at the [Paul Scherrer Institute](https://www.psi.ch/en) doing computer vision for X-ray crystallography, and then started my PhD in late 2022.
